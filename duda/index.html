Technical / Tactical

  • Breaks lines primarily through progressive passing rather than carries; progressive runs and dribble volume remain low, reinforcing her role as a distributor.
  • Positions herself constantly on the ball-side half-space, providing a stable passing angle and ensuring continuity in build-up.
  • Constantly scans before receiving, adjusting hips early and opening body shape to play forward within one or two touches, allowing her to maintain tempo even under coordinated pressure.
  • Acts as a tempo regulator: slows play to secure structure, then accelerates circulation once numerical superiority is created.
  • High progressive pass volume, although chance creation remains limited, with low xA and final-third risk-taking.
  • Defensively, relies on anticipation and positioning rather than aggressive stepping out; intercepts lanes and delays progression instead of engaging recklessly.

Psychological

  • Shows sustained work rate and disciplined adherence to tactical instructions.
  • Retains composure under pressure and avoids rushed decisions
  • Actively guides teammates by indicating lanes and structural references during build-up phases.
  • Consistently recognising moments to slow the game down or accelerate circulation through simple, high-quality decisions.
  • Consistently scans surroundings before receiving, allowing her to open body shape early and play forward under pressure.
  • Competitive and disciplined profile; follows tactical instructions precisely and sustains focus even in high-pressure matches.

Physical

  • Covers ground efficiently through constant short-to-medium movements, supporting build-up and defensive balance rather than high-intensity box-to-box runs.
  • Adjusts stride and body orientation well to remain balanced when receiving under pressure.
  • Reacts quickly in defensive transitions, allowing her to recover space and regain structure despite not relying on raw speed.

Social

  • Projects calmness and control, reinforcing her role as the organising presence in midfield.
  • No reported behavioral or cohesion issues
  • Serves as a reference point for teammates in possession, offering stable angles and calming sequences under pressure.
  • Provides on-field leadership through communication and positioning, frequently pointing teammates into passing lanes and maintaining spacing during build-up.

Summary

  • Best suited to systems focused on structured build-up, where her passing and game-reading dictate midfield control.
