Technical / Tactical

  • Aerial dominance: Strong and powerful, wins aerial duels.
  • Defensive positioning: Plays as right centre-back in both back-four and back-three systems; maintains a conservative, structured shape.
  • Ball distribution: Prefers lateral passes; struggles with accurate long balls, often turning over possession in transition.
  • Anticipation & marking: Steps up to intercept but can be passive, often waits for attackers instead of engaging early.
  • Comfort under pressure: Unsettled by intense pressure, suggests improvement needed in composure and first touch.
  • Game awareness / Spatial awareness: Needs to scan more frequently; at times loses her marker by not checking over her shoulder to track runs into her back.

Psychological

  • Passive in aggression: Not naturally physical in duels, could benefit from more assertive defending.
  • Calm but reserved: Maintains composure in routine contexts, but under sustained pressure her confidence visibly dips.

Physical

  • Tall and imposing (175 cm/5’9”), standing out clearly amongst peers.
  • Powerful duels: Uses size and strength effectively in body-on-body challenges.
  • Stride & recovery: Long leg stride grants good recovery speed over distance; less agile in tight spaces.

Social

  • Leadership in action: Uses simple gestures (“ask for head”) to direct teammates, small but effective leadership cues.
  • Quiet communicator: Low on-field vocality, but role clarity and non-verbal influence are present.

Summary

  • Marina Campillo de Oliveira is a physical and tactical prospect emerging from São Paulo’s U17 system and Brazil’s youth pathway. Her aerial presence, size (175 cm/5’9”), and positional discipline have made her a reliable defensive anchor in both back-four and back-three setups. While technically solid in short distribution, she tends to play safe, her long passing lacks accuracy and turnarounds often gift possession to opponents.
  • Tactically, she relies on good reading of the game but needs to be more assertive, too often passive in duels and reactive rather than proactive in marking. Her comfort zone doesn’t extend well to high-pressure or chaotic scenarios; improved composure and first-touch control will be crucial as she advances. Her stride and physical tools suggest solid recovery speed and duelling ability, but awareness and agility in tight situations are areas for growth.
  • Her leadership style is understated but effective, small signals and presence rather than loud vocal commands. With targeted development in aggression, technical handling, scanning, and passing range, she has the making of a reliable modern centre-back.
  • Verdict: Wonderkid , a physically advanced centre-back with solid fundamentals. With refined aggression, composure, and technical range, she’s well-positioned to make a smooth transition to senior-level football in Brazil or abroad.
